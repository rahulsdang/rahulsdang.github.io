SCS Engineers · 2024–Present
AI Tooling

AI Sourcing Tool: National Engineering Talent Map Built with Claude Code

Problem

Licensed niche talent (PE, PG, BCEE) in solid waste, landfill design, and environmental compliance does not surface reliably through standard channels. LinkedIn returns broad results, and SeekOut coverage thins out in the smaller markets where many projects sit. External search fees for hard-to-fill technical roles routinely topped $30K per hire. The recruiting function across 70+ offices and 44 states needed a comprehensive, shared map of competitor and adjacent-industry talent that the standard stack could not produce.

Action

Designed a continuously refreshed national talent intelligence map using Claude Code, built and refined over two weeks and maintained as a living resource. It ingested direct competitors, adjacent firms including Tetra Tech, Jacobs, TRC Companies, and Republic Services, municipal and county public works entities, and SWANA and RAM/SWANA conference rosters. Output lived in a single live spreadsheet, each row carrying current and prior employers, a narrative experience summary, technical specialties mapped to internal business unit needs, multi-state licensure, LinkedIn URL, and verified contact information. Every contact was tied to a specific hiring manager and business unit, so recruiters, hiring managers, and leaders could filter by specialty and act the same day.

Result

Mapped 540+ named contacts across 181 organizations. Closed 7 licensed niche engineering hires in 6 months, avoiding an estimated $200K+ in external search fees, and displaced roughly $40K+ in planned sourcing spend by removing the need for a contract sourcer and paid subscriptions. Compressed an estimated 2 to 3 months, around 270 to 405 hours, of manual sourcing into a two-week build. Adopted across recruiters, hiring managers, and executive leadership as a shared asset for sourcing, competitive talent mapping, and workforce planning.

ATS Migration: Greenhouse to iCIMS Plus Workday HRIS Integration

Problem

SCS Engineers had no real-time visibility into hiring pipeline health or retention data across 70+ offices and 44 states. Greenhouse was not integrated with their HRIS. Leaders were making workforce decisions without accurate data. Agency relationships had no accountability structure, costing significant spend annually with no measurable ROI.

Action

Launched a revamped employee referral program and introduced SeekOut for passive sourcing to reduce agency dependency across 44 states. Built custom Claude Code sourcing scripts and standardized Microsoft Copilot screening workflows to deepen internal sourcing capability across the footprint.

Result

Agency spend reduced by $250K+. Leadership gained first-ever real-time pipeline and retention visibility. Internal sourcing through SeekOut and referrals became a meaningful channel across a national footprint.

SCS Engineers · 2024–Present
Process

Intake Process Redesign: Interview-to-Offer Ratio from 5:1 to 3:1

Problem

No structured intake process existed. Hiring managers submitted reqs without clear requirements, compensation alignment, or interview frameworks. The result was a 5:1 interview-to-offer ratio: five candidates going through the full process for every single hire. Expensive in time, hiring manager bandwidth, and candidate experience across a 70-office national firm.

Action

Redesigned the intake protocol end-to-end. Implemented mandatory kickoff calls before any req went live. Built standardized job brief templates capturing requirements, compensation range, non-negotiables, and interview format. Established 48-hour feedback expectations with hiring managers. Introduced SeekOut for passive sourcing in markets where job boards were not reaching qualified candidates.

Result

Interview-to-offer ratio came down from 5:1 to 3:1 for field and technical roles within 6 months, with time-to-fill cut by 9 days across high-volume field roles. Fewer interviews, stronger candidates, faster closes.

SCS Engineers · 2024–Present
Employer Brand

Company's First Candidate Feedback Program and Employer Brand Strategy

Problem

SCS had no structured way to collect candidate feedback and no data to inform employer brand decisions. Glassdoor presence did not reflect the company's actual culture or growth trajectory. No visibility into where the candidate experience was breaking down.

Action

Launched the company's first candidate feedback program using SurveyMonkey, sent to candidates post-process regardless of outcome. Partnered with Marketing to analyze feedback, identify process bottlenecks, and translate findings into employer brand improvements including updated job descriptions, interview experience changes, and a proactive Glassdoor engagement strategy. Redesigned the career site covering mission, benefits language, and employer brand messaging.

Result

Glassdoor rating improved from 3.8 to 4.0 over 12 months. First time leadership had structured data on candidate experience. Employer brand improvements informed by real applicant feedback rather than assumptions.
